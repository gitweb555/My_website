1. Assess Your Financial Situation

  • Budget: Determine how much you can afford to spend on a home. This includes not just the mortgage payment, but also property taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs. Use a mortgage calculator to estimate monthly payments and figure out what fits within your budget.
  • Down Payment: Most mortgages require a down payment, typically ranging from 3% to 20% of the home price. The more you can put down, the better your loan terms may be. Saving for a larger down payment can also help you avoid private mortgage insurance (PMI).
  • Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI): Lenders use your debt-to-income ratio to evaluate your ability to repay a loan. A DTI of 43% or lower is ideal, but some lenders may accept higher ratios depending on your financial situation.
